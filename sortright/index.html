Know Your Bins

Each bin serves a specific purpose in the waste management ecosystem

🟢

Green Bin — Biodegradable

Organic waste that can decompose naturally and be converted into compost or biogas.

Food scraps Fruit peels Garden waste Eggshells Tea leaves Newspaper
🔵

Blue Bin — Recyclable

Dry materials that can be processed and reused to create new products, saving energy and resources.

Plastic bottles Cardboard Glass jars Metal cans Paper Electronics
🔴

Red Bin — Hazardous

Dangerous materials that require special handling and disposal to prevent harm to people and the environment.

Batteries Chemicals Medicines Paint Syringes Pesticides

Smart Disposal Tips

Small actions, massive impact

🧴

Rinse Before Recycling

Always rinse food containers before placing them in the recycling bin to avoid contaminating other recyclables.

🌿

Compost at Home

Start a small compost bin for kitchen scraps. It reduces landfill waste and creates rich fertilizer for plants.

⚠️

Never Mix Hazardous

Never put hazardous waste in regular bins. Take them to designated collection points or facilities.

📦

Break Down Cardboard

Flatten all cardboard boxes before recycling to save space and make processing easier at facilities.

🔋

Battery Drop-offs

Most electronics stores have free battery recycling drop-off points. Batteries contain toxic heavy metals.

👕

Donate Old Clothes

Textiles that can't be recycled traditionally should be donated or taken to textile recycling programs.
